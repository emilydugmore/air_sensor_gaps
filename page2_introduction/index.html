Introduction

Clean air is a basic need for enjoying a healthy life. According to the World Health Organization, almost 99% of the global population are exposed to air pollution levels that put them at increased risk for diseases, such as heart disease, stroke, chronic obstructive pulmonary disease, cancer and pneumonia. This situation has led to the outburst of sensors to monitor air quality around the world. However, the distribution of these sensors is not equal across the globe, with a significant concentration in high-income countries and a scarcity in low-income regions.
